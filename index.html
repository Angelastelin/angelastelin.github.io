MEET THE ARTIST

Angela Stelin was born and raised in Brazil, spending her childhood between the bustling São Paulo and the quiet region of Mato Grosso do Sul. Living in a family involved with music, literature, journalism, and the arts, she began to draw very early. Enthusiastic about mathematics and geometric drawing, she decided to study Industrial Design at the Armando Álvares Penteado Foundation (FAAP) in São Paulo, graduating in 1982.

Angela, her husband and children left Brazil in 1994 to live in Morocco, Turkey, Sweden, England, Switzerland, and now Portugal. In these years of adventures and challenges, she maintained her passion for art by enjoying exhibitions and manifestations of other artists wherever she went. In Istanbul, she learned new techniques such as marbling (Ebru), miniature, gouache, and watercolor.

For many years, she divided her time between the small and calm Åmål, the creative Gothenburg and the eternal vibrancy of London, developing in painting her own artistic style, expressing nature, its colors, feelings and realities. Now, living in Portugal and enjoying the sun and the beauties of the Algarve, with its colors and new elements, her stimulus is to express and transmit emotions, attraction and connection with the world.

STYLE

Fascinated by geometric figures and nature, Angela's style mixes the abstract with figurative concepts. Everyday scenes inspire the creative process, expressed on canvas using acrylic paints, tape and photographs. She enjoys the surprise when the tape is removed from the canvas, showing a concoction of figures and empty spaces, transcending between opacity and transparency, luminosity and darkness. Angela is inspired by the contradicting beauty of nature - symmetry and chaos, strength and fragility, peace and violence, light and shadow - thus becoming key components of her work.
